Chess Pieces

♟

The pawn is the least powerful chess piece, moving one square foward (up to two on its first move) and capturing diagolly. It can be promoted to any piece if it reaches the far side of the board.

♞

The knight is a unique piece in chess that moves in an L-shape: two squares in one direction (like up or down) and then one square perpendicular (like left or right), or vice versa. Knights are the only pieces that can "jump" over other pieces on the board. Each player has two knights.

♝

The bishop is a piece in chess that moves diagonally across the board. It can travel any number of squares, but only along the diagonals, staying on the same color square it started on. Each player has two bishops, one on a light square and one on a dark square.

♜

The rook is a strong piece in chess that moves in straight lines. It can move any number of squares horizontally or vertically, but not diagonally. Each player starts with two rooks, one on each corner of the board.

♛

The queen is the most powerful piece in chess. It can move any number of squares in any direction: forward, backward, sideways, or diagonally.

♚

The king is the most important piece in chess. It can move one square in any direction: forward, backward, sideways, or diagonally.
